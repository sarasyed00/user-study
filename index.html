Understanding Reciprocity in People with Autism across Peer-to-peer systems: A Brown University Study


Who You Are

  • 18 years of age or older
  • have the ability to engage in a back and forth conversation
  • have strong typing skills
  • interested in learning the basic principles of cognitive behavioral therapy and how to reframe your thinking
  • may or may not have autism
  • have access to an internet accessible tablet or computer with a video camera

What You'll Do

You will go through two remote sessions where you will communicate with an individual who is trained in peer-support and discuss something that is troubling to you. During these sessions you will be asked to share a bit about this troubling issue and you will work with the peer to identify ways to reshape your thinking to help you see your issue in a new light. During one of these sessions you will communicate via a Zoom video meeting. During the other session you will communicate via an instant messaging platform. Afterwards you will be asked to fill out a brief survey about your experience. Each session and survey should take up to an hour of your time (for a total of up to 2 hours). You may choose to opt out of the study at any point, without penalty.

After completion of the study you will receive a $40 Amazon gift card.

These sessions are not meant to substitute for therapy. Peers will not provide any diagnosis or advice.

Benefits and Risks

Potential Benefits

  • You may learn to reshape your thinking on an issue that is troubling to you.
  • You may learn the basic principles of cognitive behavioral therapy.

Potential Risks

  • Potential physical risks include eyestrain, wrist pain, etc. consistent with about 2 hours of computer usage.
  • Potential stress or discomfits that may result from the peer sessions. You may choose to discuss any issue you want during these sessions, but these sessions can be stressful or triggering, so please consider whether that is the case for you before agreeing to participate. You may stop participating in this study at any time without penalty.

Research goals

We are researchers at Brown University's Human Computer Interaction Lab who are studying online peer-to-peer support systems. Creating a connection to your peer is an important part of the efficacy of peer-support systems. As such, we want to learn how to better design internet based systems to improve user’s comfort with sharing and reciprocating, in order to better forge connections on online support systems. We also want to learn how to design for accessibility, so we are interested in involving both neurotypical individuals and individuals with Autism/Aspergers in our study.
